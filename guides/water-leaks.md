# Water Leaking Inside — Troubleshooting Guide

Water pooling around your indoor AC unit is one of the most common service calls in South Florida. The good news: the most frequent cause is a clogged drain line, which you can fix yourself.

## Understanding the Problem

Your AC removes moisture from the air as part of the cooling process. In South Florida, a typical system can remove **5-20 gallons of water per day** from indoor air. This water collects in a drain pan and flows out through a condensate drain line.

When that line clogs, the water backs up and overflows.

## Step 1: Turn Off the System

Before doing anything, shut off the AC at the thermostat. This stops condensation from accumulating while you work.

## Step 2: Locate the Drain Line

The condensate drain is a PVC pipe (usually 3/4 inch diameter) that exits your home near the outdoor unit or into a floor drain. The drain pan is under the evaporator coil inside the air handler.

## Step 3: Check for Clogs

**At the exit point outside:**
- Is water dripping? If nothing comes out when the AC runs, the line is clogged.
- Is there visible algae or slime at the opening?

**At the air handler:**
- Is the drain pan full of standing water?
- Is the PVC pipe connection secure?

## Step 4: Clear the Clog

### Method 1: Vinegar Flush
1. Locate the drain line access point (T-fitting or cleanout cap near the air handler)
2. Pour 1 cup of white vinegar into the line
3. Wait 30 minutes
4. Flush with warm water
5. Check the exit point — water should flow freely

### Method 2: Wet/Dry Vacuum
1. Go to the exit point of the drain line outside
2. Attach a wet/dry vacuum hose to the drain line opening
3. Seal the connection with a rag or tape
4. Run the vacuum for 30-60 seconds
5. Check the vacuum canister — you should see water and possibly algae/slime

### Method 3: Compressed Air (careful)
1. Low-pressure compressed air at the access point can break up clogs
2. Use short bursts only
3. **Do not use high pressure** — you can crack the PVC or blow apart fittings

## Prevention

In South Florida's humid climate, drain line maintenance is not optional:

- **Monthly**: Pour 1 cup of vinegar down the drain line
- **Quarterly**: Flush with warm water after the vinegar
- **Annually**: Have a technician inspect and clean the drain pan, check the float switch, and verify the line is clear

## When to Call a Pro

- Water is coming from the unit itself (not the drain pan) — possible frozen evaporator coil
- The drain pan is cracked
- You smell mold near the unit
- The float switch has triggered and the system won't restart after clearing the line
- Water damage has occurred to walls, ceiling, or flooring
