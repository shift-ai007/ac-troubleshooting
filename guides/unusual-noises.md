# Unusual AC Noises — Troubleshooting Guide

Your AC should produce a steady, low hum when running. Anything else is a signal that something needs attention. Here's what each noise means and how urgently you need to act.

## Noise Identification

### Banging or Clanking
**What it sounds like:** Metallic banging, like something loose hitting something else
**Where it comes from:** Usually the outdoor unit

**Likely causes:**
- Loose or broken connecting rod inside the compressor
- Loose fan blade hitting the protective cage
- Debris inside the outdoor unit (branch, rock, tool)

**Urgency:** HIGH — Turn off the system. A loose component can cause cascading damage.
**DIY?** Check for visible debris in the outdoor unit. If the fan blade is bent or loose, call a pro.

### Squealing or Screeching
**What it sounds like:** High-pitched metallic squeal, often at startup
**Where it comes from:** Either indoor or outdoor unit

**Likely causes:**
- Worn fan belt (older systems with belt-driven blowers)
- Failing blower motor bearings
- Compressor under high head pressure

**Urgency:** MEDIUM — The system can usually run short-term, but the failing part will eventually seize.
**DIY?** No. Motor and belt replacement requires a technician.

### Clicking
**What it sounds like:** Repeated clicking, especially when the system is trying to start
**Where it comes from:** Outdoor unit or thermostat area

**Likely causes:**
- Failing relay or contactor
- Defective thermostat
- Electrical control issue

**Urgency:** MEDIUM-HIGH — If the system won't start and keeps clicking, the capacitor or contactor may be failing.
**DIY?** Check thermostat batteries. Beyond that, call a pro — capacitors are dangerous.

### Buzzing
**What it sounds like:** Electrical buzzing or humming, louder than normal operation
**Where it comes from:** Outdoor unit

**Likely causes:**
- Failing contactor (the relay that sends power to the compressor)
- Loose wiring
- Debris touching the condenser fan
- Failing compressor motor (at end of life)

**Urgency:** MEDIUM — Often indicates an electrical component nearing failure.
**DIY?** Clear any visible debris from the outdoor unit. For electrical buzzing, call a pro.

### Hissing
**What it sounds like:** Steady hiss or whistle
**Where it comes from:** Refrigerant lines or indoor unit

**Likely causes:**
- Refrigerant leak (most common)
- Expansion valve operating normally (brief hiss at startup is OK)
- Duct leak near the air handler

**Urgency:** HIGH if continuous — A refrigerant leak means losing cooling capacity and potential compressor damage.
**DIY?** No. Refrigerant work requires EPA certification. Call a licensed tech.

### Gurgling or Bubbling
**What it sounds like:** Water bubbling, like a coffee maker
**Where it comes from:** Near the indoor unit

**Likely causes:**
- Condensate drain line partially clogged (air bubbles in trapped water)
- Refrigerant leak (liquid refrigerant boiling in the line)

**Urgency:** LOW-MEDIUM — Try flushing the drain line first. If the noise persists, call for a refrigerant check.
**DIY?** Flush the drain line with vinegar. If noise continues, it's not the drain.

## Summary Table

| Noise | Likely Cause | Urgency | DIY? |
|-------|-------------|---------|------|
| Banging | Loose part, debris | High | Check debris only |
| Squealing | Motor bearings, belt | Medium | No |
| Clicking | Capacitor, relay | Medium-High | Check thermostat |
| Buzzing | Contactor, wiring | Medium | Clear debris |
| Hissing | Refrigerant leak | High | No |
| Gurgling | Drain clog, refrigerant | Low-Medium | Flush drain |

## General Rule

If the noise is **new**, **loud**, or **getting worse**, turn off the system and call a professional. Running a damaged AC can turn a $300 repair into a $3,000 replacement.

---

*Tip: Record the noise on your phone before calling a technician. The sound often disappears by the time they arrive, and a recording helps with diagnosis.*
