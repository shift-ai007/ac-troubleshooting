# AC Blowing Warm Air — System On But Air Feels Warm

This is frustrating — the system is running, you can hear it, but the air coming out feels like room temperature or warm.

## Diagnose Step by Step

### 1. Thermostat Fan Setting
Check if the thermostat fan is set to **ON** (continuous) vs **AUTO**. If set to ON, the blower runs constantly but the cooling may cycle on and off. The air between cooling cycles is just room-temperature air. Switch to AUTO and wait 10 minutes.

### 2. Heat Pump in Heat Mode
During spring/fall in Florida, a heat pump can get stuck in heating mode or fail to switch over. Check the thermostat mode — should say COOL, not HEAT or AUTO (if AUTO selects the wrong mode).

### 3. Outdoor Unit Not Running
If the outdoor condenser isn't running but the indoor blower is, you'll get warm air. Go outside and check:
- Is the fan spinning? No → possible [failed capacitor](guides/unusual-noises.md)
- Is the unit humming but not starting? → bad capacitor or contactor
- Nothing at all? → tripped breaker or blown fuse

### 4. Reversing Valve Stuck (Heat Pumps Only)
Heat pumps use a reversing valve to switch between heat and cool. If it sticks in the heat position, the system blows warm air in cool mode. This requires a technician to diagnose and repair.

### 5. Low Refrigerant
If the system is running at full speed but blowing slightly-cool-but-not-cold air, refrigerant charge is likely low. This is the most common cause of "running but not cooling" once basic checks pass.

## Quick Decision

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Action |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Fan on ON, not AUTO | Wrong setting | Switch to AUTO |
| Outback unit fan not spinning | Bad capacitor | Call a pro |
| Both units running, air barely cool | Low refrigerant | Call a pro |
| Warm air, heat pump, no outdoor ice | Reversing valve | Call a pro |
