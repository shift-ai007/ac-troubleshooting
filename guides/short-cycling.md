# AC Short Cycling — Diagnosis & Solutions

Short cycling means your AC turns on and off in rapid cycles (less than 10 minutes at a time) instead of running properly through full cooling cycles. This wastes energy, raises your electric bill, strains the compressor, and eventually leads to premature system failure.

## Step 1: Identify the Pattern

Listen to a few cycles before diagnosing. The specific timing tells you what's wrong:

| Pattern | Likely Cause | Action |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Runs 2-5 minutes, shuts off, restarts quickly | Oversized system, thermostat placement, or dirty filter | Check filter first, then thermostat location |
| Runs 5-10 minutes, shuts off, long pause | System is reaching set temp but short cycle suggests airflow restriction | Check filter and coils |
| Runs 2-3 minutes, clicks off, won't restart | Thermal overload — compressor is overheating | Call a professional immediately |
| Runs 1-2 minutes, shuts off, restarts after freeze-up | Frozen evaporator coil from low refrigerant or airflow | Turn system off, check filter, call a pro |

## Step 2: Easy DIY Checks (Do These First)

### Check the Air Filter

A clogged air filter is the most common cause of short cycling. Restricted airflow causes the evaporator coil to get too cold, triggering the safety limit switch.

1. Locate your air filter (return vent or air handler cabinet)
2. Hold it up to light — if you can't see through it, it's time to replace
3. Replace with the correct size filter (check the frame for dimensions)
4. Run the system and see if cycles normalize

### Check the Thermostat

A thermostat in a bad location tricks the system into short cycling:

- **Near a heat source**: Thermostat next to a lamp, TV, or appliance reads warm, tells AC to keep running even though the house is cool
- **In direct sunlight**: Sun heats the thermostat directly, same problem
- **Near a supply vent**: Cold air blows directly on the thermostat, telling it the house is cold when it's not

**Quick test**: Tape a thermometer near the thermostat and compare. If they disagree by more than 2 degrees, you may have a placement issue.

### Check the Breaker and Disconnect

A tripping breaker that resets and immediately trips again indicates a short or ground fault. DO NOT keep resetting — this can cause electrical fire.

## Step 3: When to Call a Professional

If the DIY checks didn't resolve it, the remaining causes require a licensed HVAC technician:

### Low Refrigerant Charge
When refrigerant is low, the evaporator coil gets partially starved. The coil temperature drops below freezing, ice forms, airflow decreases, and the system overheats. The high-pressure switch trips, shutting the system down. When it cools enough, the switch resets and the cycle repeats.

**Homeowner test**: If the large refrigerant line (suction line, usually the larger of the two copper lines at the outdoor unit) has ice on it, you likely have a refrigerant issue. Turn the system off and call a pro.

### Oversized AC System
An oversized system cools the air quickly but doesn't run long enough to remove humidity. South Florida homes with oversized units feel cold and clammy because the system short-cycles, never hitting the 20-30 minute runtime needed for dehumidification.

**How to tell**: Your system cools the house to set temperature in under 10 minutes on a 95-degree day. It should take 20-30 minutes of continuous runtime.

### Failed Components

| Component | Symptom |
|-----------|---------|
| **Capacitor** | Motor tries to start, hums, shuts off — repeats |
| **Pressure switch** | System runs briefly, clicks off, resets after a few minutes |
| **Thermal overload** | Compressor heats up, shuts off, restarts after cooling |
| **Control board** | Erratic cycling with no consistent pattern |

### Refrigerant Pressure Safety Switch

Your system has a low-pressure and high-pressure safety switch. When either trips:

1. The system shuts down immediately (protecting the compressor)
2. The switch auto-resets after the pressure equalizes
3. The system restarts and the cycle repeats

This looks like "short cycling" but is actually a safety feature protecting the compressor from damage.

## Prevent Short Cycling

- **Change air filters monthly** — especially during high-use months (May-October in South Florida)
- **Keep outdoor unit clear** — 2 feet clearance on all sides, no tall grass or debris
- **Schedule annual maintenance** — a professional tune-up catches issues before they cause problems
- **Consider a variable-speed system** — Inverter-driven ACs ramp up/down instead of cycling on/off, eliminating short cycling entirely
