# SEER Ratings Explained

SEER stands for **Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio**. It measures how efficiently an AC system converts electricity into cooling over an entire cooling season.

## What the Numbers Mean

**SEER = Total cooling output (BTU) / Total electrical input (Wh) over a season**

Higher SEER = more efficient = lower electricity bills.

| SEER Rating | Efficiency Class | Typical Monthly Cost* | Annual Savings vs 10 SEER |
|-------------|-----------------|----------------------|--------------------------|
| 10 | Minimum (pre-2015) | $210 | — |
| 13 | Old federal minimum | $162 | $576/year |
| 14 | Previous FL minimum | $150 | $720/year |
| 15 | Current FL minimum | $140 | $840/year |
| 16 | High efficiency | $131 | $948/year |
| 18 | Very high efficiency | $117 | $1,116/year |
| 20+ | Ultra efficiency | $105 | $1,260/year |

*Estimates based on a 3-ton system running 2,800 hours/year in South Florida at $0.13/kWh*

## SEER2 — The New Standard (2023+)

As of January 2023, new AC equipment uses **SEER2** ratings. SEER2 uses updated testing conditions that better reflect real-world performance (higher static pressure).

**Approximate conversion:** SEER2 ratings are roughly 4-5% lower than SEER for the same equipment.

- Old: 15 SEER minimum for Florida
- New: 14.3 SEER2 minimum for Florida (split systems)

## What SEER Should You Buy?

For South Florida, where AC runs nearly year-round:

- **15-16 SEER**: Good value for most homeowners. Reasonable upfront cost with solid efficiency.
- **18+ SEER**: Worth it if you plan to stay in the home 10+ years. The higher upfront cost is offset by lower bills over time.
- **20+ SEER**: Premium systems with variable-speed compressors. Best humidity control and quietest operation, but highest upfront cost.

## The Diminishing Returns Problem

The jump from 10 SEER to 16 SEER saves about $80/month. The jump from 16 SEER to 22 SEER saves about $25/month. Each additional SEER point saves less than the last.

**Rule of thumb for South Florida:** 16 SEER hits the sweet spot of efficiency vs cost for most homeowners.

## Beyond SEER: What Else Matters

SEER doesn't capture everything:

- **Humidity removal** — Variable-speed systems remove more moisture even if SEER is similar
- **Part-load performance** — How well the system operates when it doesn't need full capacity
- **Duct condition** — A 20 SEER unit with leaky ducts performs like a 14 SEER unit
- **Sizing** — An oversized high-SEER unit will still short-cycle and underperform

---

*Always get a Manual J load calculation before choosing equipment size. SEER only matters if the system is correctly sized for your home.*
