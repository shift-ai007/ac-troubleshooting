# AC Not Cooling — Troubleshooting Guide

Your AC is running but no cold air comes out. Follow these steps in order.

## Step 1: Check the Thermostat

- Is it set to **COOL** (not HEAT or FAN ONLY)?
- Is the set temperature **below** room temperature by at least 3 degrees?
- Are the batteries fresh? (if battery-powered)
- Try setting it to 5 degrees below current room temp and wait 5 minutes.

**If thermostat seems fine, move to Step 2.**

## Step 2: Check the Air Filter

- Locate your return air vent (usually in a hallway or near the unit)
- Remove the filter and hold it up to light
- If you **cannot see light through it**, the filter is clogged

**A clogged filter is the #1 cause of cooling failures.** Replace it and wait 30 minutes for the system to recover.

In South Florida, 1-inch filters should be replaced **monthly** during summer. The high humidity causes filters to collect more particulate, faster.

## Step 3: Check the Outdoor Unit

Go outside to your condenser unit:

- **Is it running?** You should hear the compressor and see the fan spinning.
- **Is it blocked?** Clear any vegetation, debris, or objects within 2 feet of the unit.
- **Are the coils dirty?** If the aluminum fins are visibly caked with dirt, gently rinse with a garden hose (from inside out).

**If the outdoor unit is not running at all, check the breaker** (see below).

## Step 4: Check the Breaker Panel

Your AC typically has **two breakers** — one for the air handler (indoor) and one for the condenser (outdoor).

- Open your electrical panel
- Find the breakers labeled AC, A/C, HVAC, or AIR HANDLER
- If either is in the middle position (tripped), flip it fully OFF, wait 30 seconds, then flip it ON

**Important:** If the breaker trips again immediately, **stop and call a professional**. Repeated tripping indicates a serious electrical fault.

## Step 5: Check the Drain Line

A full condensate drain pan can trigger a safety float switch that shuts down cooling.

- Find the drain line (PVC pipe near the indoor unit, usually 3/4 inch)
- Check if water is draining freely at the exit point
- If clogged, flush with a cup of white vinegar or use a wet/dry vacuum on the exit end

## When to Call a Pro

Call a licensed technician if:

- The breaker trips repeatedly
- The outdoor unit hums but the fan doesn't spin (possible bad capacitor)
- You see ice on the refrigerant lines
- The system runs but air temperature at the vent is not at least 15-20 degrees cooler than room temperature
- You've checked everything above and the problem persists

---

*Safety note: Never open the electrical compartment on your AC unit. Capacitors can hold lethal voltage even when the system is off.*
