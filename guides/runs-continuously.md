# AC Runs Continuously — Unit Never Shuts Off

An AC system that never stops running is wearing out faster and costing you money. Here's why it happens and what to do.

## Normal vs Problematic Run Cycles

In South Florida summer (90°F+, high humidity), a properly sized AC runs **50-70% of the time** — about 12-16 hours per day during peak season. It should cycle ON and OFF, not run continuously.

If your AC has been running for 3+ hours without stopping, something is wrong.

## Causes

### 1. Thermostat Fan Set to ON
The most common "problem" that isn't a problem. Check the thermostat Fan setting:
- **AUTO** — Fan runs only when cooling
- **ON** — Fan runs 24/7 regardless of cooling
- Switch to AUTO and see if the system starts cycling normally

### 2. Unit Is Undersized
If your AC is too small for your home, it will run continuously trying to keep up with the heat load. In South Florida, undersizing is less common than oversizing — unless an addition or sunroom was built without upsizing the system.

Check: On a 90°F day, is the indoor temperature slowly dropping (not keeping up) or holding steady? If holding steady, the system is keeping up — it's just running longer than expected.

### 3. Thermostat in Wrong Location
If the thermostat is in the coolest room (e.g., a north-facing hallway with no windows), the rest of the house could be warm while the thermostat is satisfied. Move the thermostat or use room sensors.

### 4. Low Refrigerant
An undercharged system runs longer because the air coming out isn't cold enough to satisfy the thermostat. Check for [ice buildup](guides/ice-buildup.md) on the pipes — that's the telltale sign.

### 5. Duct Leaks
If conditioned air is leaking into the attic through disconnected ducts, the system runs continuously trying to cool a space that can never reach setpoint. This wastes significant energy.

### 6. Poor Insulation or Air Leaks
Check for:
- Open windows or doors
- Gaps around window AC units
- Poor attic insulation (Florida recommends R-38 or higher)
- Fireplace damper left open

## What Continuous Run Costs

| Runtime Increase | Extra Monthly Cost (S. FL) |
|-----------------|---------------------------|
| Normal (12h/day) | Baseline |
| 16h/day | +$50-80 |
| 24h/day | +$150-250 |

If you're running continuously AND the house isn't cooling, call a technician. You're paying for full operation with little benefit.
