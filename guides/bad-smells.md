# Bad Smell from Vents — Musty, Burning, or Chemical Odors

A bad smell from your AC vents is a signal that something needs attention. Different smells point to different problems.

## Smell Type Quick Reference

| Smell | Likely Cause | Urgency |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Musty/moldy | Mold in ducts or on coil | Medium |
| Burning | Electrical issue or dust burn | High |
| Rotten eggs | Gas leak or dead animal | Immediate |
| Chemical/sweet | Refrigerant leak | High |
| Sewage | Dry P-trap or sewer gas | Medium |
| Fishy/electrical | Overheating wire or component | Immediate |

## Musty or Moldy Smell

The most common AC odor in South Florida. High humidity means condensate lines and drain pans stay wet year-round.

**Causes:**
- **Wet drain pan** — Algae and mold grow in standing water. Pour 1 cup of undiluted white vinegar down the access tee on the drain line once a month to prevent this.
- **Dirty evaporator coil** — Dust + moisture = mold. Requires professional cleaning.
- **Mold in ducts** — Especially in flex duct with tears or poor insulation. Professional duct cleaning can help.
- **Dirty air filter** — A filter that's been wet or in place too long can grow mold.

**Fix:**
1. Replace the air filter
2. Clean the drain line with vinegar
3. Consider a UV air purifier installed in the ductwork — UV light kills mold growth on the coil
4. Schedule [duct cleaning](https://ac-repair.today/services/duct-cleaning/) if the smell persists

## Burning Smell

**Immediate action:** If the smell is sharp or acrid, turn off the system at the breaker and call a technician. This could be an electrical fire risk.

**Non-emergency:** A mild burning smell the first time you turn on the heat in fall is normal — dust burning off the heat exchanger. It should clear within 30 minutes.

## Chemical or Sweet Smell

A sweet, chloroform-like smell means refrigerant is leaking into your home. This can cause health symptoms (headache, nausea, dizziness) and will eventually destroy your AC system.

**Action:** Turn off the AC, open windows, and call a licensed HVAC technician. Do not run the system until it's repaired.

## Preventing Odors

- Change air filters every 1-3 months
- Pour 1 cup vinegar down the drain line quarterly
- Have the evaporator coil inspected during annual maintenance
- Keep the area around the air handler clean and dry
