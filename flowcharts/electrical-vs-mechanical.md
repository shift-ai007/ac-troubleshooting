# Electrical vs Mechanical — Narrow Down the Failure Type

Use this guide to determine whether your AC problem is electrical or mechanical. This helps communicate clearly with your technician and understand repair quotes.

## Electrical Problems

### Symptoms
- System won't start at all
- Starts but stops immediately
- Intermittent operation
- Clicking sounds but no fan/compressor

### Common Electrical Failures
| Component | Expected Life | Cost to Replace |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| Capacitor | 5-10 years | $150-250 |
| Contactor | 5-10 years | $100-200 |
| Transformer | 10-15 years | $150-300 |
| Blower motor | 10-15 years | $400-800 |
| Control board | 10-15 years | $300-600 |
| Compressor | 10-15 years | $1,500-3,000 |

### Why You Shouldn't DIY Electrical
Capacitors store 300+ volts even when power is off. Control boards are sensitive and easily damaged. Faulty wiring can cause fires. Always use a licensed technician.

## Mechanical Problems

### Symptoms
- Runs but doesn't cool properly
- Makes unusual sounds
- Vibrates excessively
- Water leaks

### Common Mechanical Failures
| Component | Expected Life | Cost to Replace |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| Fan motor (outdoor) | 8-12 years | $300-600 |
| Fan motor (indoor) | 10-15 years | $400-700 |
| Compressor valves | 8-12 years | $1,500-3,000 |
| TXV/expansion valve | 10-15 years | $400-800 |
| Bearings | 5-10 years | $200-500 |
| Refrigerant recharge | Varies | $200-800 |

## Mixed Electrical + Mechanical

Some failures involve both systems:

- **Compressor won't start** — Electrical (capacitor) + mechanical (seized bearings)
- **Blower weak but running** — Electrical (motor winding) + mechanical (dirty wheel)
- **Short cycling** — Electrical (thermostat) + mechanical (oversized unit or dirty filter)

## Communicating with Your Technician

When you call for service, report:
1. What it's doing (won't start / runs but warm / strange sounds)
2. What you've already checked (filter, breaker, thermostat)
3. How old the system is
4. Any recent repairs or maintenance

This saves diagnostic time and helps the tech bring the right parts.
