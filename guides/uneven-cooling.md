# Uneven Cooling — Some Rooms Cold, Others Warm

Uneven temperatures between rooms is one of the most common homeowner complaints, especially in two-story homes and South Florida open floor plans.

## Common Causes (Least to Most Expensive)

### 1. Closed or Blocked Vents
Check every room. Make sure supply registers are open and nothing is blocking them. Even one closed vent in a room can unbalance the system.

### 2. Dampered Duct Branches
Look in the attic or crawlspace for a manual damper lever on the main duct branches. These are sometimes set wrong. Adjust toward rooms that need more cooling.

### 3. Duct Leaks
A disconnected or torn duct in the attic dumps cold air into the attic instead of your rooms. Feel for cold spots in the attic or look for obvious tears.

### 4. Room Placement Factors
- **South-facing rooms** get more direct sun and need more cooling
- **Rooms above garages** lose heat through the floor
- **Rooms with lots of windows** have higher heat gain
- **Second floors** are naturally warmer (heat rises)

### 5. Manual J Design Issues
Many homes have a single AC system serving zones with very different cooling loads. In South Florida, a west-facing great room with vaulted ceilings on a different zone than east-facing bedrooms is the classic mismatch.

## Solutions

| Issue | Solution | Cost |
|-------|----------|------|
| Closed vents | Open them | Free |
| Wrong damper setting | Adjust | Free |
| Duct leak | Tape or mastic seal | $50-200 |
| Poor insulation | Add attic insulation | $500-2000 |
| Undersized duct | Replace/upsize duct run | $300-800 |
| Wrong zone design | Zone dampers or mini-split | $1500-5000 |
