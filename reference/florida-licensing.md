# Florida HVAC License Lookup — How to Verify Your Contractor

Florida is one of the few states with strict HVAC licensing. Here's how to verify your contractor and what each license type means.

## Why It Matters

Unlicensed AC repair is a serious problem in Florida. A licensed contractor has:
- Passed state competency exams
- Carried workers' compensation insurance
- Posted a bond protects you from incomplete work
- Continuing education (every 2 years for licenses, every year for certifications)

If an unlicensed contractor is injured on your property, you may be liable. If they damage your system, you have no recourse.

## License Types

| License | Scope | Issued By |
|---------|-------|-----------|
| CAC (Class A) | Commercial AC — any size system | DBPR |
| CAC (Class B) | Residential AC — up to 30 tons | DBPR |
| CFC (Class A) | Commercial refrigeration | DBPR |
| CFC (Class B) | Residential refrigeration | DBPR |
| Registered | Work done under supervision of certified contractor | Local municipality |

**The most common license for residential AC work is CAC 1814427 (Class B).** Always ask for the license number.

## How to Verify

### Online (Fastest)
1. Go to: **https://www.myfloridalicense.com/wl11.asp**
2. Click "Search for a License"
3. Select "Contractor - Air Conditioning" from the dropdown
4. Enter the contractor's license number or name
5. Check the status field — it must say "CLEAR/ACTIVE"

### By Phone
Call DBPR: **(850) 487-1395**

### What to Check
- **Status**: Must be "CLEAR/ACTIVE" — not "SUSPENDED", "REVOKED", or "DELINQUENT"
- **Qualifying Agent**: The person whose license backs the company
- **Insurance**: Ask if they carry workers' comp and general liability
- **Expiration**: Is the license current?

## Red Flags

- Contractor can't or won't provide a license number
- License shows "INACTIVE" or "SUSPENDED"
- Contractor asks you to get the permit
- "Cash only" discount on repairs over $500
- No physical address (PO box only)
- High-pressure tactics — "sign today for 20% off"

## Florida Statutes

Under Florida law:
- All AC contractors must hold a valid license for jobs over $500 (total cost, not just labor)
- Contractors must pull permits for system replacement
- Consumers have a 7-day right to cancel for door-to-door sales
- Warranty work must be completed in a reasonable time frame
