# AC Won't Turn On — No Response from System

When your AC won't turn on at all, start with the simplest possible causes and work your way up.

## Step-by-Step

### 1. Thermostat Power
- **Battery-powered**: Replace batteries. Many thermostats show a low-battery warning, but not all.
- **Hardwired**: Check the breaker. If the thermostat screen is blank or flickering, the 24V transformer may have failed or a safety switch may be open.

### 2. Circuit Breaker
- Check the main panel for a tripped breaker labeled AC or Condenser
- Don't just flip it back — if it trips again immediately, there is a short circuit
- Also check the small disconnect box near the outdoor unit (pull the handle out, wait 30 seconds, push back in)

### 3. Safety Switches
Modern AC systems have safety switches that kill power to prevent damage:

- **Float switch** — In the drain pan or drain line. If the drain is clogged, water rises and trips the switch, shutting down the system. Look for water in the drain pan.
- **High-pressure switch** — Trips if refrigerant pressure gets too high. Resets automatically once the system cools down. If it trips repeatedly, there's an underlying issue.
- **Thermal overload** — Protects the compressor from overheating. It auto-resets in 30-60 minutes.

### 4. Failed Components
| Component | Symptom | Fix |
|-----------|---------|-----|
| Capacitor | Humming but nothing starts | Professional replacement |
| Contactor | Clicking but no power to unit | Professional replacement |
| Transformer | Thermostat dead (hardwired) | Professional replacement |
| Compressor | Humming, hot, won't start | Professional evaluation |
| Control board | Nothing responds | Professional replacement |

### 5. Condensate Overflow
If your float switch tripped for a reason, the drain may be clogged. Try clearing the primary drain line:
- Find the PVC pipe near the indoor unit
- Pour 1 cup of white vinegar down the access tee
- Wait 30 minutes for the algae/sludge to dissolve
- Flush with water

If water was in the pan, the system should restart after the float switch dries.

## When to Call

Try these checks first. If none of them works, the issue is electrical or mechanical and requires a licensed technician.
